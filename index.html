CustomerReview

Cris Xander O. Salas

Honestly, nothing beats the classics. I know a lot of people are into fusion food and all that fancy stuff, but for me, isaw and banana cue will always hit differently. Isaw—whether it's manok or baboy—has this smoky, savory flavor that just slaps.

Kurt Baterbonia

Buko is the ultimate refresher. Ice-cold coconut juice straight from the shell, with soft strips of buko meat? You don’t need soda or fancy bottled drinks when nature already made this masterpiece. It’s refreshing, hydrating, and feels like a tropical vacation in a cup—especially on a hot day. Plus, it’s actually healthy. Like, guilt-free sarap.

Christine De Asis

Calamares hits the spot when you want something crunchy but juicy. Those deep-fried squid rings, golden and slightly chewy, dipped in sweet chili or vinegar sauce? Instant mood booster. And when it’s cooked right (aka not rubbery), it's chef’s kiss. It’s like the Filipino version of calamari, but with more street soul.

Juris Chin Sorongon

Fishball is the definition of “simple pero panalo.” It’s not fancy, it’s not trying to impress—it’s just there, floating in hot oil, waiting for you to stab it with a stick and drown it in that legendary sweet, spicy, or vinegar sauce. It’s not just food—it’s a ritual.
